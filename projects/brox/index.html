Overview

What I built

Offline-first Flutter desktop workspace operations app for coworking/business-space owners and front-desk teams. It manages check-ins, live sessions, checkout, customers, rooms, desks, kitchen inventory, reservations, analytics, finance/staff, memberships, data export/restore, roles, permissions, audit log, app health, and local release packaging.

Built a broad local-first operations console with business rules, persistent repositories, role-gated workflows, and release documentation.

Verified current context reports 531 Dart files under lib/src, 98 Dart test files, 520 widgets, local schema version 3, and local datasets for customers, sessions, reservations, rooms, consumables, finance, memberships, roles, and audit logs.

Highlights

What makes it notable

Broad operations scope

Covers check-ins, live sessions, checkout, customers, rooms, desks, kitchen inventory, reservations, analytics, finance, staff, memberships, roles, permissions, audit logs, app health, and local recovery.

Local-first operating model

Release-oriented engineering

Engineering

Decisions and constraints

Decision 01

Keeping persistence out of the UI

Repositories and Cubit/Bloc boundaries keep the desktop UI focused on workflows instead of direct storage manipulation.

Decision 02

Making admin workflows recoverable

Backup, restore, diagnostics, audit logs, and app health are treated as product features, not afterthoughts.

Decision 03

Designing for repeated front-desk use

Navigation and screens prioritize dense operational scanning, quick actions, and clear role-gated paths.
